## Brief structure — 5 segments, ~4-6 min total

Write five segments. Each segment is spoken audio (160 wpm ≈ 2.7 words/sec).
Target seconds per segment are guides, not strict caps; the TOTAL should land
within 240-360 seconds.

1. **sources_audit (~45s)** — Name 3-4 primary sources the module draws
   from. One short sentence per source on why each is trustworthy for this
   topic (regulator / standards body / peer-reviewed / etc.). Lead with
   the source the listener will recognize most.

2. **outline_preview (~75s)** — Walk the listener through the module's
   arc. Hook one-liner → core claim → name the sub-concept titles in
   sequence → integration → end with "and you'll wrap with N self-checks
   to test what stuck." Audio-native, not a table of contents read aloud.

3. **corpus_connections (~30s)** — Name 1-2 items from the listener's
   corpus that this module connects to (article titles or sources they've
   read). If CORPUS_SNIPPETS_JSON is empty or the connections are weak,
   say so honestly ("this module doesn't lean heavily on your prior
   reading — it's foundational material").

4. **high_stakes_claims (~90s)** — Surface 2-3 most consequential factual
   claims the module makes, each WITH its cited source named inline.
   These are the claims a listener should evaluate critically.

   **If S7_VERDICT is `revise` or `reject`**, this segment MUST honestly
   flag the specific defects S7_RATIONALE names. Don't oversell a flawed
   module. Example phrasing: "Heads-up: the verification pass flagged
   that the module attributes X to the CFA Institute — that attribution
   couldn't be verified. Worth keeping in mind when you hear that section."

   **If any S6 claims are still flagged as needs_human_review**, surface
   the most material one with the same heads-up framing.

5. **sample_clip (~60s)** — Verbatim ~150-word excerpt from the MIDDLE of
   the module transcript. Pick a passage from the middle sub-concept beat
   that sounds good standalone — a beat with a clear hook, not a
   transition. DO NOT paraphrase; copy the prose exactly.

## Drafting rules

- **Audio-native register.** Same conversational, listener-second-person
  voice as the full module. Pacing cues ("here's the key bit", "about
  halfway through"). No headers read aloud.
- **In-line source naming.** "Per the SEC's 2024 staff guidance…" not
  "according to one source." Same convention as the module itself.
- **No new facts.** The brief draws only from the transcript + S6/S7
  trail + citations. Don't invent context.
- **Honest about defects.** If S7 said revise, the brief says so plainly
  in the high-stakes-claims segment. The listener decides; don't decide
  for them by hiding flaws.
- **No marketing language.** No "you'll love this," "incredibly important,"
  or "everyone should know." Just orient the listener and let them choose.
