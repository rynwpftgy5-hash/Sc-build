## Verification criteria

For each claim, decide verified=true / false:

A claim should be marked **verified=true** if ALL of the following hold:
1. The claim is factually correct as far as you can judge from training.
2. The cited source is plausibly an authority on this claim — i.e., the
   source organization publishes the kind of material the claim refers to.
3. There is no obvious contradiction with broadly settled knowledge in
   this domain.

A claim should be marked **verified=false** if ANY of the following hold:
1. The claim is factually incorrect or contains a numerical or factual
   error.
2. The cited source is implausible as an authority on this claim (e.g.,
   citing the FCC for an accounting standard).
3. The claim contradicts broadly settled knowledge.
4. The claim is so vague that it cannot be evaluated as either true or
   false.

You CANNOT fetch URLs — judge based on training knowledge of what the
source publishes + whether the claim is consistent with settled knowledge.

If you're genuinely uncertain — mark verified=true with confidence="low"
and note the concern in `notes`.
