## Rubric (score 0-10 each, half-points allowed)

1. **structural_fit** — Does the causal mechanism in the source analog map
   onto the target topic? Score high when the mechanism's structure (actors,
   incentives, sequence of effects, feedback loops) maps cleanly; low when
   the mapping requires significant force-fitting or when only the surface
   features rhyme.

2. **confidence** — How confident are you in this mapping? Account for: how
   well you understand the analog itself; how well you understand the target;
   how settled the comparison is in the literature you've seen. Distinct from
   structural_fit (a fit can be tight but uncertain, or loose but obvious).

3. **epistemic_strength** — How well-established is the SOURCE ANALOG's OWN
   causal claim in the literature? Score 8-10 for `settled` (consensus in
   serious economics/policy/history of technology), 4-7 for `contested`
   (multiple coherent positions in the literature; still a live debate),
   0-3 for `speculative` (one author's framing not yet absorbed; recent
   journalism / blogposts; "as some have argued" territory).

4. **constitutive_role** — Was the causal element NECESSARY to the source
   outcome (constitutive — scores 7-10) or merely CONTRIBUTORY/AMPLIFYING
   (scores 0-6)? Constitutive means: remove this element and the outcome
   doesn't happen. Contributory means: it shaped pace/scale but the
   outcome would have arrived through other channels.

## Composite score
Composite = (structural_fit + confidence + epistemic_strength + constitutive_role) / 4

Top-1 or top-2 analogs by composite (≥6.0) advance to drafting at S5.
Weaker analogs (<5.0) MUST be cut and called out explicitly in the
Sources-audit segment of the review brief.
