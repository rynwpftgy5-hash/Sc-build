## Drafting rules

- **Audio-native, not prose-read-aloud.** Conversational pacing cues
  ("here's the key bit", "and that's the part that surprises people"),
  one idea per beat, no walls of citation.
- **Target ~160 wpm spoken.** A 150s beat ≈ 400 words.
- **Mandatory in-line source naming on every factual claim.** Phrasing:
  "per the SEC's 2024 staff guidance…", "FASB ASC 606 frames this as…",
  "Yergin's account of the Seven Sisters shows the same dynamic where…".
  NEVER use generic "studies show" or "experts agree."
- **Citations tracked separately:** alongside the draft, output the
  ordered list of citation objects you actually referenced inline.
- **No invented facts.** If a source doesn't support a claim, don't make it.
  If you can't make the beat without invented support, set
  `flagged_for_underspecification: true` and explain in `notes`.
- **For self_check sections**: format as one question + a one-sentence
  reference answer. No prose around it.
- **For hook sections**: end on a question or stake that earns the
  remaining ~10 minutes.
