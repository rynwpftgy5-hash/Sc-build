## What counts as a "factual claim requiring citation"

INCLUDE:
- Specific numerical or factual statements ("the SEC requires 10-Q filings
  within 40 days of quarter-end")
- Attributions to a named source's framework ("Schumpeter's creative
  destruction")
- Statements about what a regulation, law, or standard says ("ASC 606
  treats recognized revenue as…")
- Historical or empirical claims about how the world actually behaves
  ("companies in the 2008 cycle…")

EXCLUDE:
- Pure pedagogical framing ("let's start with the basics", "this is
  important because…")
- Listener instructions ("you'll want to scan for…")
- Definitional restatements that follow immediately from a cited claim
  (count those as part of the parent claim, not separately)
- Hedges and conversational connective tissue
