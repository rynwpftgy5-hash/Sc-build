## Context

What is the issue that motivates this decision? Describe the forces at
play — technical, organisational, single-principal workflow concerns,
constraints from upstream systems, etc. State the problem in terms
that will still make sense to a reader who has no memory of the
originating conversation.

If this ADR is migrating a §8.4a.* dispatch, summarise the dispatch
trigger condition and the state of the system at the moment the
decision was made.

## Decision

The decision itself, stated in active voice and present tense
("SpaceSC adopts X for Y because Z"). Be specific. Avoid weasel words.
If the decision has multiple sub-parts, enumerate them.

If there are explicit alternatives that were considered and rejected,
list them under a brief "Considered alternatives" subsection so future
readers do not re-litigate the same options.

## Consequences

What becomes easier? What becomes harder? What new obligations does
the team (i.e. Campbell + the agents) take on? What follow-up
decisions does this force, and where will those live?

Use bullet points. Separate positive consequences from negative
consequences when both are non-trivial.

## Implementation notes (optional)

Phased build sequence, acceptance criteria, pause-for-Campbell gates,
or other operational detail. Keep this section short — anything longer
than ~one screen belongs in `OPS_PLAYBOOK` or in a follow-up dispatch.

## Open questions (optional)

Items consciously deferred. Each entry should name the workstream
where the question will be resolved.
